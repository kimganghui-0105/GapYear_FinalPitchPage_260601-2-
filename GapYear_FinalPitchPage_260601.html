Section 011. Game Title & Tagline

Core Experience

Players spend a virtual year (50 weeks) exploring an analog world with complete freedom. By documenting the journey through an analog lens, players find deep emotional release, mindfulness, and a sense of self-discovery.

Target Platform: PC (Steam) Exclusive
Specifications & Genre

Genre: Healing, Photography, Life & Sabbatical Simulation
Platform Goal: Developed exclusively for PC (Steam) to deliver immersive environmental graphics and tactile, granular manual controls for camera operations.

The Design Philosophy

Section 022. Purpose of the Game

"How do we discover ourselves through diverse experiences,
and what is the true healing value of a temporary pause?"

• The Core Question: Can a structured period of complete, un-incentivized rest help a person re-evaluate their identity in a hyper-competitive society?

• Why It Matters: Modern burnout stems from a lack of intentional pausing. This game serves as a safe psychological laboratory where taking a break does not equal falling behind.

• Why a Game? Novels tell stories of wandering; films show others doing it. A game grants Player Agency. Deciding whether to linger at a train station all day, converse with a local, or capture a sunset gives players personal ownership over their time and memories.

Section 033. Narrative, World & Characters

• Story Premise: Exhausted by daily routine, the protagonist visits a dim studio for an official photo [Choice: Exam / Job / Other]. The room's ambient items and dialogue shift dynamically based on this choice. As the camera flash goes off, an enigmatic photographer lends the player a vintage manual camera, opening a rift to an unconstrained year-long sabbatical.

• World: A dual-layered open world. It starts with highly researched, realistic open spaces filled with ambient noise (Paris, Kyoto) and evolves into mystical historical/fantasy worlds as the player engages with local cultural quests.

Section 044. Gameplay & Core Interaction

Player Role & Objective

The player acts as a 'Wanderer and Observer' stepping away from reality. The goal is to compile a highly personalized, artistic photo diary over 50 virtual weeks, uncovering psychological insights and personality leanings through the archive.

Core Mechanics & Challenges
  • • Rarity & Timing: Capturing precise moments when an NPC displays genuine, brief emotional actions under shifting weather patterns.
  • • Resource Limitations: Finite film rolls introduce an intentional artistic tension, preventing careless button-mashing.
  • • Stamina Management: Managing fatigue forces a balance between aggressive exploration and deep, meditative downtime.
Win / Lose / End Conditions

There are no Game Over screens, failures, or deaths. The game concludes naturally when 50 virtual weeks pass or if the player opts to select the 'Return Home' option via the smartphone UI. Endings generate a tailored psychological evaluation letter out of 16 distinct archetypes based on a statistical analysis of your captured photos, social ties, and preferred settings.

Economy System

Photos transferred to the base's vintage terminal are evaluated for compositional uniqueness and exchanged for local currencies. Players use these funds to purchase transit tickets (train, flight) to new zones or customizable furniture and decor to turn their base into a welcoming retreat.

Monetization: Premium Model
Zero Pay-To-Win: Pure Premium Package Release

To maintain the meditative atmosphere of our gameplay, disruptive in-app purchases, microtransactions, and gacha mechanisms (Free-to-Play models) are entirely omitted. We utilize a straightforward Premium Pay-Once Package model, granting permanent and complete access to all systems without artificial mechanical gates.

Analytical Ending Matrix Example

• 'Global Voyager' Route: Unlocked by constant traversal across multiple territories, culminating in an accumulated collage of postcards sent from global acquaintances.

Section 066. Aesthetics & UI

Visual Style

A warm pastel palette combined with low-poly 3D environmental assets maximizes mental clarity and relaxation. Upgrading lenses overlay faint analog film grain effects onto the camera screen.

Audio Direction

Centered on acoustic guitar progressions and ambient Lo-Fi tracks. Moving from busy hubs to natural zones dynamically fades out musical accompaniment, accentuating white noise like waves and rustling leaves.

UI Design (No-HUD UX)

All typical onscreen HUD metrics—like progress bars and persistent minimaps—are removed. Essential metrics are integrated cleanly into a minimalist smartphone interface accessed by pressing the Tab key.

Section 088. Reference Games & Positioning Map

We analyze three successful titles to establish our market positioning, building upon their core strengths while solving specific limitations.

Minecraft

Similarities: High user autonomy without an overbearing narrative framework, turning space into a personal 'place'.
Our Innovation: Mitigates choice paralysis by introducing a non-intrusive aesthetic guide system that anchors the player's short-term motivation.

Sky: Children of the Light

Similarities: Relies on synesthetic environments rather than heavy expository text, treating traversal as a reward.
Our Innovation: Limits repetitive grinding loops by applying an absolute 50-week chronological framework.

Outer Wilds

Similarities: Self-driven exploration where curiosity leads to insight, complemented by musical rest spots.
Our Innovation: Swaps punishing physics and lethal time limits for soft psychological profiling and emotional exploration.
